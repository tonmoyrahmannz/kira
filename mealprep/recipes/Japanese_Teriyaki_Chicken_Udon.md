# Japanese Teriyaki Chicken Udon (High-Fibre Family Version)

## Cuisine
Japanese

## Servings
4

## Cook time
45 minutes

## Pregnancy safety notes
- Chicken is cooked through fully before serving.
- No raw egg or unpasteurised ingredients.

## Why this recipe is high fibre
- Adds edamame + carrots + optional broccoli for extra fibre.
- Can use wholegrain/extra-fibre udon where available.

## Ingredients
- Chicken thigh, 650 g (bite-size)
- Udon noodles, 2 packs (~700 g)
- Carrots, 2 medium (julienned)
- Frozen shelled edamame, 1.5 cups
- Optional broccoli florets, 2 cups
- Onion, 1 small sliced
- Garlic, 3 cloves minced
- Ginger, 1 tbsp grated
- Soy sauce, 4 tbsp
- Mirin, 2 tbsp
- Oyster sauce, 1 tbsp
- Brown sugar, 1 tsp
- Oil, 1.5 tbsp
- Spring onion + sesame seeds to garnish

## Step-by-step cooking instructions
1. Cook udon as packet directs; rinse and set aside.
2. Mix soy, mirin, oyster sauce, sugar, 3 tbsp water.
3. Stir-fry onion, garlic, ginger in oil (2 min).
4. Add chicken, cook until fully done (6–8 min).
5. Add carrots, edamame, broccoli; stir-fry 3–4 min.
6. Add sauce + noodles, toss 2–3 min until glossy.
7. Garnish with spring onion.

## Kid friendly serving notes
- Keep sauce mild; serve chilli separately.
- Cut chicken and veg small for easy eating.

## Leftover reuse suggestions
- Great for next-day lunchbox thermos (short-cut noodles if needed).
