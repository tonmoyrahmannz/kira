# Turkish Beef Kofta Tray Bake with Chickpea Salad

## Cuisine
Turkish

## Servings
4

## Prep time
20 minutes

## Cook time
30 minutes

## Total time
50 minutes

## Pregnancy safety notes
- Kofta are baked until fully cooked through.
- Yogurt used should be pasteurised.

## Why this recipe is high fibre
- Chickpeas + salad vegetables increase fibre.
- Serve with wholemeal pita for extra whole grains.

## Ingredients

### Pantry ingredients
- Ground cumin, 1 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tsp
- Coriander powder, 1/2 tsp
- Salt, pepper
- Oil, 2 tbsp

### Fresh ingredients to buy
- Beef mince, 700 g
- Onion, 1 small (grated)
- Garlic, 3 cloves
- Parsley, 1 bunch
- Chickpeas, 2 cans (400 g each)
- Cucumber, 1
- Tomato, 3
- Red onion, 1/2
- Lemon, 1
- Plain yogurt (pasteurised), 500 g
- Wholemeal pita, 1 pack

## Step-by-step cooking instructions
1. Heat oven to 220°C.
2. Mix beef mince, grated onion, half garlic, cumin, paprika, coriander, salt, pepper, chopped parsley.
3. Form oval kofta and place on oiled tray.
4. Bake 18–22 minutes until browned and fully cooked.
5. For salad: rinse chickpeas, combine with diced cucumber, tomato, red onion, lemon juice, oil, salt.
6. Mix yogurt with remaining garlic and pinch salt.
7. Serve kofta with chickpea salad, yogurt sauce, and warm pita.

## Kid friendly serving notes
- Serve Sophie small kofta pieces with plain yogurt dip.
- Keep onion minimal in her portion if needed.

## Storage notes
- Kofta keep 3 days refrigerated.
- Salad keeps 1–2 days (best fresh).

## Leftover reuse suggestions
- Kofta wraps for next-day lunch.
- Crumble leftover kofta into tomato pasta sauce.

## Optional substitutions
- Swap beef for chicken mince.
- Use lettuce wraps instead of pita.
