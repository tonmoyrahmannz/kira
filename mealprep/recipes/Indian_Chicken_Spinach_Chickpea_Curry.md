# Indian Chicken, Spinach & Chickpea Curry

## Cuisine
Indian

## Servings
4

## Prep time
15 minutes

## Cook time
35 minutes

## Total time
50 minutes

## Pregnancy safety notes
- Chicken is simmered until fully cooked.
- No undercooked eggs or unpasteurised ingredients.

## Why this recipe is high fibre
- Chickpeas and spinach significantly increase fibre.
- Serve with brown rice for extra fibre.

## Ingredients

### Pantry ingredients
- Oil, 2 tbsp
- Canned tomatoes, 1 can (400 g)
- Indian spices (garam masala, turmeric, cumin, coriander)
- Rice, for serving

### Fresh ingredients to buy
- Chicken thigh, 700 g (bite-size)
- Onion, 1 large
- Garlic, 4 cloves
- Ginger, 1 tbsp
- Chickpeas, 1 can (400 g)
- Baby spinach, 200 g
- Greek yogurt (pasteurised), 1/2 cup (optional swirl)
- Fresh coriander, small bunch

## Step-by-step cooking instructions
1. Cook rice.
2. Heat oil, sauté onion until soft.
3. Add garlic, ginger, and spices; cook 1 minute.
4. Add chicken and brown lightly.
5. Add canned tomatoes + 1/2 cup water; simmer 20 minutes.
6. Add drained chickpeas; simmer 8 minutes.
7. Stir in spinach until wilted.
8. Optional: swirl in yogurt off heat. Garnish with coriander.

## Kid friendly serving notes
- Blend part of sauce for smoother texture if Sophie prefers.
- Serve mild with rice and cucumber side.

## Storage notes
- Refrigerate up to 3 days.
- Freezes well up to 2 months.

## Leftover reuse suggestions
- Use in wraps with lettuce for adult lunch.
- Pack with rice in thermos for daycare lunch.

## Optional substitutions
- Replace chickpeas with red lentils (cook longer with extra water).
- Use chicken breast if preferred.
