## 1. Vision Statement

Build a career path from Data Architect to Principal Data Architect and ultimately Head of Data / Head of Data & AI, while establishing Whiro Analytics as a respected independent advisory practice.

The objective is **not** to become an expert in every technology. The objective is to become a trusted advisor who can evaluate, select, govern, lead, and communicate enterprise data platforms and teams — and charge accordingly.

**Target positioning by 2029–2031:**

> _"A strategic Data & AI leader who helps organisations modernise data platforms, improve governance, enable analytics and AI, and deliver measurable business outcomes — as a Principal Architect, Head of Data, or Fractional Data Leader."_

## 4. Next 90 Days: Detailed Plan (Jun–Aug 2026 — revised)

**Note:** You feel ready for both DP-700 and DP-600 given your hands-on Fabric experience. This plan is condensed — take practice tests first to confirm, then book exams accordingly.

### Priority 1: DP-700 — Fabric Data Engineer (Implementing Data Engineering Solutions)

**Why DP-700 first?** This is your daily work — data loading, pipelines, lakehouses, warehouses, orchestration, PySpark. It validates what you already do as the data platform lead at VUW. Highest-ROI certification for your current role.

**Why it's the right cert for you:** DP-700 covers data loading patterns, data architectures, orchestration, ingestion & transformation, and monitoring/optimisation — the exact skillset of a Data Architect designing Fabric platforms.

**Effort estimate:** 15–25 hours total (given 2 years hands-on Fabric experience)

**Study plan (condensed):**

| Week | Focus | Effort |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Take a practice exam first. Target weak areas: Spark workspace settings, domain settings, monitoring/optimisation specifics | 3–4 hrs |
| 2 | Data ingestion patterns, pipelines, orchestration, data warehouse implementation | 3–4 hrs |
| 3 | Real-time intelligence, environment management, Activator | 3–4 hrs |
| 4 | Practice exams, gap analysis, sit exam | 3–4 hrs |

**Take a practice exam immediately** — your 2 years of hands-on work means you likely hit 70-80% already. Focus only on the gaps.

### Priority 2: DP-600 — Fabric Analytics Engineer (Implementing Analytics Solutions)

**Why DP-600 second:** Semantic models, DAX optimisation, Direct Lake, Power BI integration, governance. You know this too, but it's more analytical/BI — less your daily architect work, still valuable for Whiro consulting and full-stack Fabric credibility.

**Effort estimate:** 10–20 hours total

**Study approach:**
| Week | Focus | Effort |
|------|-------|--------|
| 5 | Practice exam. Target: DAX specifics, semantic model optimisation, composite models | 3–4 hrs |
| 6 | Governance, deployment pipelines, XMLA endpoints, large semantic models | 3–4 hrs |
| 7 | Practice exams, gap analysis, sit exam | 3–4 hrs |

### Priority 3: AZ-305 — Azure Solutions Architect Expert

**Why:** The architect certification that signals seniority. Covers compute, networking, storage, security, migration, resilience — forces architect-level thinking beyond Fabric.

**Effort estimate:** 30–40 hours total over 6 weeks

## 5. Broader Learning Plan (Beyond 90 Days)

### Technology Priority Ranking

**Tier 1 — High priority (invest first)**

| Technology | Why | When | Effort Estimate |
|------------|-----|------|-----------------|
| DBT | Industry standard for data transformations. Every modern data team uses it. You need it on your CV. | Jan–Feb 2027 | 20–30 hrs to production-comfortable |
| PostgreSQL | Growing NZ market share, open-source alternative to SQL Server. VUW may use it. | Mar–Apr 2027 | 15–20 hrs for functional depth |
| Data Governance / Purview | Differentiator for senior roles. Few architects have real governance experience. | Apr–Jun 2027 | 20–30 hrs (depends on project exposure) |

**Tier 2 — Medium priority (add after Tier 1)**

| Technology | Why | When | Effort |
|------------|-----|------|--------|
| Snowflake Architecture | Awareness-level for comparison. Not urgent unless a specific opportunity needs it. | Jul–Aug 2027 | 15–20 hrs |
| Databricks Fundamentals | Complementary to Fabric. Key for AI/ML pipelines. | Sep–Oct 2027 | 15–20 hrs |
| AWS Data Platform | Second cloud awareness (S3, Redshift, Glue). Important for consulting breadth. | Nov–Dec 2027 | 20–25 hrs |

**Tier 3 — Nice to have (pick up opportunistically)**

| Technology | Why | Effort |
|------------|-----|--------|
| Fivetran | Replication-tool awareness. Easy to learn if a project uses it. | 5–10 hrs |
| ERWIN / Data modeling tools | Formal data modeling. SQL Server Data Tools already covers this partially. | 10–15 hrs |

### Learning Philosophy

1. **Learn what you use.** The best learning happens while solving real problems at VUW. Every new concept should be applied within 2 weeks or it's forgotten.
2. **Depth over breadth.** One platform you can architect deeply is worth more than three you've read about.
3. **Portfolio over certificates.** A documented case study of "how I solved X using Y" is worth more than any certification.
4. **Don't chase shiny objects.** If a new tool launches, wait 6 months. If it's still relevant, learn it then.

## 6. Portfolio & Case Study Targets

Your portfolio is what gets you the next role. Certifications open doors; case studies close deals.

### VUW Case Studies (to document)

1. **Data Platform Architecture** — the overall design, choices made, trade-offs
2. **Governance Implementation** — what you put in place and why
3. **Migration / Modernisation** — moving from legacy to Fabric
4. **Cost Optimisation** — measurable savings or efficiency gains

### Format per case study

- Problem statement
- Options considered (with trade-offs)
- Decision and rationale
- Architecture diagram (Excalidraw or similar)
- Outcome (quantified where possible)
- Lessons learned

### Where to publish

- LinkedIn articles (start here — lowest friction, highest visibility)
- Own blog / Substack (once you have 3+ pieces)
- Conference talk abstracts (NZ SQL User Group, Data Day, Fabric Community)
