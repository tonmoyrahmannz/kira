# Sales Partner / Referral Process

*For Tonmoy's technical sales friend — a simple, repeatable process for identifying and referring leads.*

---

## Who This Is For

A technical sales contact (or multiple) who:
- Works in or adjacent to NZ IT/data/analytics
- Talks to people who might need Power BI, Fabric, or reporting help
- Can warm-intro Tonmoy to decision-makers
- Wants a clean, simple commission arrangement — not a complex partnership

---

## What Makes a Good Referral

The best referrals are organisations where someone says any of these:

| Trigger Phrase | Best Offer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Our Power BI reports are so slow" | Power BI Health Check | Easy entry, low commitment |
| "I don't trust the numbers in our dashboards" | Power BI Health Check | Pain is real, need is clear |
| "We're thinking about Fabric..." | Fabric Readiness Assessment | Strategic, bigger deal |
| "This one report takes hours to update" | Reporting Modernisation Sprint | Hands-on, visible win |
| "Our BI person left and nobody knows how the reports work" | Health Check + Sprint | Clean-up + rebuild |

## Referral Process (for the Partner)

### Step 1: Spot the Signal
You hear someone mention slow reports, messy data, Fabric confusion, or a reporting pain point.

### Step 2: Quick Qualifier (30 seconds)
Ask: *"Who looks after your reporting and data?"*
- If they say "nobody really" or "we could use some help" → it's a lead

### Step 3: Warm Intro
Send a WhatsApp/LinkedIn/email to Tonmoy with:
> **Contact name:** 
> **Org:** 
> **What they said:** 
> **Permission to reach out?:** Yes/No

Or if they're keen, offer to set up a 3-way intro:
> *"I know someone who does exactly this — Tonmoy from Whiro Analytics. Would you be open to a quick call with him?"*

### Step 4: Tonmoy handles the conversation
Discovery call, scoping, proposal. Partner stays looped in.

### Step 5: Deal closes
Whiro Analytics delivers. Commission paid.

## Commission Model

**Simple flat commission:**

| Deal Size (ex GST) | Commission |
|---|---|
| Under $5,000 | $500 flat fee |
| $5,000–$10,000 | $1,000 flat fee |
| $10,000–$20,000 | 10% of deal value |
| Over $20,000 | 10% of deal value (capped at $5,000) |

**Terms:**
- Commission paid within 14 days of Whiro Analytics receiving payment from client
- Referral must be documented in writing before initial contact with the prospect
- No commission on repeat business unless the partner continues to actively manage the relationship
- One-time referral fee per new client — not a recurring commission

**Partner does not:**
- Set pricing or scope
- Make commitments on Tonmoy's behalf
- Submit proposals or sign contracts

## Referral Agreement (Short Version)

*A simple email or message that formalises the arrangement:*

> Hi [Partner Name],
>
> Thanks for helping with referrals for Whiro Analytics. This is our simple arrangement:
>
> **You refer** — someone who needs Power BI, Fabric, or reporting help. Warm intro or contact detail with permission.
> **I handle** — discovery, proposal, delivery, and invoicing.
> **You earn** — $500–$5,000 flat commission depending on deal size (see schedule).
>
> Commission is paid after I receive payment from the client. Referral must be documented before first contact.
>
> No ongoing commission unless you're actively managing the relationship.
>
> Sound good? Reply to confirm and we're set.

## Sales Partner Expectations

- Expected: 1–2 qualified leads per quarter (low volume, high quality)
- Not expected: cold calling, proposals, client management
- Best partners are: consultants, IT vendors, MSPs, ex-colleagues in data roles, or people who talk to CFOs/CIOs regularly

## Notes for Kira

- Keep partner/referral lead status in the Whiro Analytics Microsoft Lists lead tracker.
- Send Tonmoy a reminder if a referred lead has been sitting in "Intro sent" for >2 weeks without follow-up.
- The flat fee model keeps it simple — avoid percentage-only models for small deals.
- If the partner starts bringing multiple leads, discuss moving to a retainer or higher percentage.
