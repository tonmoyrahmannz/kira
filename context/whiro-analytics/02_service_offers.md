# Whiro Analytics — Initial Consulting Offers

**Legal entity:** WHIRO IT LIMITED (trading as Whiro Analytics)  
**Trading brand:** Whiro Analytics  
**Positioning:** Modern reporting, data platforms, and analytics strategy for NZ organisations.

---

## Core Positioning

Whiro Analytics helps organisations move from slow, manual, fragmented reporting to trusted, governed, and scalable analytics using Microsoft Power BI, SQL, and Microsoft Fabric.

The business is best positioned as a specialist Microsoft data and analytics consultancy, not a general IT support provider.

**Primary client problems:**

- Power BI reports are slow, fragile, or hard to maintain.
- Reporting logic is buried in Power Query, Excel files, or undocumented dataflows.
- Business teams do not trust numbers across different reports.
- Data models, workspaces, permissions, and refreshes have grown without governance.
- Organisations are considering Microsoft Fabric but do not know where to start.
- Internal IT teams are too busy or do not have dedicated data architecture/data engineering capability.
- Previous consultants delivered something but left poor documentation or no support model.

**Core promise:**

> From messy reporting to trusted analytics.

# Offer 1: Power BI Health Check

## Purpose

A focused review of an organisation’s current Power BI environment to identify performance issues, governance gaps, reporting risks, and quick-win improvements.

This offer is designed as a low-friction entry point for new clients.

## Best for

Organisations that:

- Already use Power BI but are struggling with performance, refreshes, reliability, or trust.
- Have important dashboards owned by one or two key people.
- Have reports built from Excel, Power Query, SQL, SharePoint, dataflows, or multiple unmanaged sources.
- Need an independent view before investing in a larger reporting or data platform project.
- Are unsure whether their current Power BI setup is scalable.

## Typical scope

Review selected areas such as:

- Workspace structure and ownership
- Dataset/semantic model design
- Data refresh performance and failure points
- Power Query and DAX complexity
- Report usability and duplication
- Security and access approach
- Gateway and source connectivity risks
- Data lineage and documentation
- Governance and deployment practices
- Licensing/capacity considerations where relevant

## Deliverables

- Power BI Health Check findings report
- Risk and issue register
- Quick wins list
- Recommended remediation roadmap
- Optional executive summary for leadership
- Optional backlog of improvement tasks for internal teams or Whiro Analytics to deliver

## Suggested engagement model

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks  
**Effort:** 2–5 consulting days depending on size  
**Commercial model:** Fixed-fee assessment

## Indicative pricing

- Small environment / one critical reporting area: **NZD $3,000–$5,000 + GST**
- Medium environment / multiple reports or workspaces: **NZD $6,000–$10,000 + GST**
- Larger environment: scoped separately

## Example sales wording

> We can review your current Power BI setup and identify what is slowing it down, where the reporting risks are, and what practical improvements would make the biggest difference. The outcome is a short, actionable roadmap rather than a long theoretical report.

## Conversion path

This offer can lead into:

- Report optimisation
- Semantic model rebuild
- Dataflow/Power Query refactoring
- SQL/Fabric backend implementation
- Reporting Modernisation Sprint
- Ongoing fractional analytics support

# Offer 2: Fabric Readiness Assessment

## Purpose

A strategic and technical assessment to help organisations decide whether, when, and how to adopt Microsoft Fabric.

This is positioned as an architecture and roadmap offer, not just a licensing discussion.

## Best for

Organisations that:

- Use Microsoft 365, Power BI, Azure SQL, Synapse, Data Factory, or legacy data platforms.
- Are hearing about Microsoft Fabric but are unsure how it fits.
- Have fragmented reporting and want a modern data platform.
- Are preparing for a business case, RFI, RFP, or vendor selection.
- Need client-side architecture advice before engaging a large implementation partner.
- Want to avoid over-engineering or adopting an outdated architecture pattern.

## Typical scope

Review selected areas such as:

- Current data sources and reporting landscape
- Existing Power BI architecture
- Data integration patterns
- Data warehouse/lakehouse maturity
- Security, governance, and access model
- Data engineering capability
- Microsoft licensing and capacity considerations
- Suitability of Fabric components such as Lakehouse, Warehouse, Data Factory, semantic models, notebooks, pipelines, and OneLake
- Migration priorities and sequencing
- Risks, constraints, and implementation options

## Deliverables

- Fabric readiness report
- Current-state observations
- Recommended target-state architecture
- High-level roadmap and sequencing
- Candidate workloads for migration
- Risks and dependency register
- Implementation options: internal team, partner-led, hybrid, or phased
- Optional architecture diagram
- Optional RFI/RFP input or review

## Suggested engagement model

**Duration:** 2–4 weeks  
**Effort:** 5–10 consulting days depending on complexity  
**Commercial model:** Fixed-fee assessment or time-and-materials

## Indicative pricing

- Light readiness review: **NZD $7,500–$12,000 + GST**
- Standard assessment with roadmap and architecture: **NZD $12,000–$20,000 + GST**
- Enterprise/vendor-selection support: scoped separately

## Example sales wording

> Microsoft Fabric can simplify a lot of the modern data platform stack, but it needs to be adopted with the right architecture and governance. We help you understand whether Fabric is the right direction, what should move first, what should stay as-is, and what a practical roadmap should look like.

## Conversion path

This offer can lead into:

- Fabric proof of concept
- Data platform architecture design
- Lakehouse/Warehouse implementation
- Power BI migration
- Vendor-side or client-side architecture support
- Ongoing fractional Data Architect support

# Offer 3: Reporting Modernisation Sprint

## Purpose

A hands-on delivery sprint that modernises one high-value report, dashboard, or reporting process.

This is designed to prove value quickly by improving performance, maintainability, reliability, and trust.

## Best for

Organisations that:

- Have a business-critical report that is slow, fragile, or manual.
- Depend on Excel/Power Query/dataflows that have become hard to maintain.
- Have refreshes that fail or take too long.
- Need to move logic from reports into a proper SQL/Fabric/data-engineering layer.
- Want a practical proof point before committing to a broader data platform programme.

## Typical scope

A sprint may include:

- Review of current report and data flow
- Identification of bottlenecks and duplicated logic
- Redesign of backend data model
- SQL stored procedures/views or Fabric Warehouse/Lakehouse implementation
- Power Query refactoring or removal where appropriate
- Semantic model cleanup
- Report performance improvements
- Documentation and handover
- Deployment recommendations

## Deliverables

- Modernised reporting pipeline for one agreed use case
- Updated Power BI report and/or semantic model where in scope
- Refactored SQL/Fabric objects where in scope
- Before/after performance comparison where measurable
- Technical documentation
- Support handover notes
- Recommended next backlog items

## Suggested engagement model

**Duration:** 2–6 weeks  
**Effort:** 5–20 consulting days depending on complexity  
**Commercial model:** Fixed-fee sprint for clearly defined scope, or time-and-materials for uncertain environments

## Indicative pricing

- Small sprint / one report optimisation: **NZD $6,000–$10,000 + GST**
- Standard modernisation sprint: **NZD $12,000–$25,000 + GST**
- Complex reporting process: scoped separately

## Example sales wording

> Instead of starting with a large platform programme, we can modernise one painful reporting process first. This gives you a measurable improvement, a better technical pattern, and a practical foundation for future reporting work.

## Conversion path

This offer can lead into:

- Wider reporting migration
- Data engineering backlog
- Fabric implementation
- Power BI governance setup
- Ongoing managed reporting support
- Fractional analytics leadership

# Recommended First Sales Motion

## Step 1: Lead with the Health Check

The Power BI Health Check should be the easiest first offer to sell. It is specific, low-risk, and appeals to organisations that already know they have reporting pain.

## Step 2: Use Fabric Readiness for strategic buyers

Use this when speaking to CIOs, IT managers, digital transformation leaders, CFOs, or organisations planning a data platform investment.

## Step 3: Convert pain into a sprint

Once a client shows you a broken or painful report, offer a Reporting Modernisation Sprint.

## Suggested first outreach message

Hi [Name],

I’ve recently set up Whiro Analytics to help NZ organisations modernise reporting and data platforms using Power BI, SQL, and Microsoft Fabric.

A common problem I’m seeing is that organisations have Power BI reports that are business-critical but slow, fragile, hard to govern, or built on top of messy data flows.

I’m offering a focused Power BI Health Check that reviews the current reporting setup and provides a practical roadmap of quick wins, risks, and longer-term improvements.

Would it be worth a short conversation to see whether this could be useful for your team?

Thanks,  
Tonmoy
