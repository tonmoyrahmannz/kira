# Front Yard Garden Context

## Location

Front yard of the house in Aotea, Porirua, Wellington, New Zealand.

The front yard consists of:

* A long rectangular lawn strip approximately 14m long × 2m wide.
* A concrete pathway running alongside the house.
* An established hedge running parallel to the black metal fence.
* Modern grey stone and white weatherboard house exterior.
* Good visibility from:

  * Front entrance
  * Street
  * Driveway
  * Living area windows

The front garden is primarily ornamental rather than productive.

## Tulip Planting Strategy

The available tulips are NOT sufficient to fill the entire 14m lawn strip.

Instead:

* Plant in a concentrated feature display.
* Use grouped drifts rather than straight lines.
* Keep colours grouped together.
* Avoid alternating colours.

Recommended layout:

1. Strong Gold (yellow) group
2. Snowboard (white) group
3. Ile de France (red) group

The goal is to create a strong focal point rather than sparse flowers spread over a large area.

## Maintenance Preferences

Owner preferences:

* Low maintenance.
* High visual impact.
* Seasonal colour is welcome.
* Avoid plants that become invasive.
* Avoid plants requiring constant pruning.
* Prefer perennial or self-returning plants where practical.

Kira should proactively:

* Remind about bulb planting windows.
* Suggest fertilising schedules.
* Suggest dividing bulbs if overcrowded.
* Suggest seasonal planting opportunities.
* Suggest watering only when conditions require it.
